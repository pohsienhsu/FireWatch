Brief: This analysis plot shows acres burned across the range of total dates in the fires dataset.

Features: FIRE_YEAR, FIRE_SIZE

Type: Line Chart

Packages: sklearn, seaborn, matplotlib, numpy, pandas

Data: Fires Dataset, Years 1992-2015

Description: This analysis plot shows acres burned across the range of total dates in the fires dataset. The trend line is aggregated across all years, and shows a strong linear correlation between time and millions of acres burned. From this, we can observe that the fire intensities escalate as the years advance, and our choropleth should reflect this. It seems to peak in 2005 and 2012, and it can be seen on our map for those years that in fact an alarmingly high frequency of high intensity fires (i.e. fire size >200 and <490, one even hit 998.0 size) did in fact occur during these time periods to create this effect. Thus, our visualizer accurately reflects our observations.
