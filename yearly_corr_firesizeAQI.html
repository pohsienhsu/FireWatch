Yearly Correlation (Fire Size vs. Average AQI) 


Brief: These analysis plots show the yearly correlation between average AQI (measured over each fire's duration) and the size of each fire over the range of dates (i.e. 1992-2015).
Features: FIRE_SIZE --> Size of each fire considered 
    	  AVG_AQI --> AQI level averaged over the entire duration of the fire
Packages: sklearn, seaborn, matplotlib, numpy, pandas
Type: Scatterplots
Description: These analysis plots show the yearly correlation between average AQI (measured over each fire's duration) and the size of each fire over the range of dates (i.e. 1992-2015). They provide slightly different results from the fire sum to AQI comparisons. Any fire of "irrelevant" size, (i.e. <=30) was discarded, and yearly plots for roughly 3000 fires are shown here. While this does reveal slightly visible correlation (observe the "left-to-right" motion of the data points), it is obvious that such correlation can be improved by adding better features (see Clustering), because the direct influence of fire size on AQI is not enough.
